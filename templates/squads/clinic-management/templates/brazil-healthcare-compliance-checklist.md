# Brazilian Healthcare Compliance Checklist — Clinic Operations
# Clinic Management Squad — Private Beta
# Covers: Lei 8.080/90, CFM CEM 2019, ANS RN 566/2022, ANVISA, LGPD, Estatuto do Idoso

## Patient Rights (Lei 8.080/90 + CFM CEM 2019)

- [ ] Patient informed consent documented before any procedure (CEM 2019 Art. 22)
- [ ] Patient medical records maintained for minimum 20 years per CFM Res. 1.821/2007
- [ ] Patient has right to access their own medical records on request
- [ ] Patient dignity and privacy protected throughout clinic visit
- [ ] Emergency care provided regardless of health plan status (Lei 8.080/90 Art. 7)

## Elderly Patient Rights (Estatuto do Idoso — Lei 10.741/03)

- [ ] Priority scheduling queue active for patients aged 60 or older (Art. 15)
- [ ] Priority service at all touchpoints for elderly patients (Art. 3)
- [ ] Accessible facilities for mobility-limited elderly patients
- [ ] Minimum priority slots allocated per session (document count)
- [ ] Staff trained on elderly patient rights obligations

## Physician Registration & Scope (CFM)

- [ ] All attending physicians have active CRM registration verified
- [ ] Procedures performed only within physician's registered specialty scope (CEM Art. 96)
- [ ] Resident physicians supervised per CFM Res. 2.221/2018
- [ ] Medical record signed by responsible physician within required timeframe
- [ ] Telemedicine sessions comply with CFM Res. 2.314/2022

## Health Plan Billing (ANS + TISS)

- [ ] All procedure codes validated against current TUSS/TISS standard
- [ ] Fee table loaded and verified against active convênio contract
- [ ] Authorization obtained before elective procedures requiring pre-auth
- [ ] Emergency authorization requested within ANS RN 566/2022 timelines (2h maximum)
- [ ] Glosa contestation submitted within 30-day regulatory window
- [ ] ANS RN 566/2022 patient appeal rights information provided on denial

## LGPD Patient Data Protection (Lei 13.709/18)

- [ ] Legal basis for patient data processing documented (Art. 11 — health care)
- [ ] Data Processing Officer (DPO) appointed and contact published
- [ ] RIPD (Relatório de Impacto à Proteção de Dados) current and filed
- [ ] Patient consent obtained for data sharing with third parties
- [ ] Data minimization applied — collect only what is clinically necessary
- [ ] Data retention policy documented and enforced
- [ ] Patient data deletion/portability rights process in place
- [ ] Data breach notification procedure documented (ANPD reporting within 2 working days)

## ANVISA Facility Requirements

- [ ] Clinic registered with ANVISA (Alvará Sanitário) — current and valid
- [ ] Sterilization procedures comply with RDC 15/2012 (sterilization of medical devices)
- [ ] Medical waste disposal complies with RDC 222/2018
- [ ] Pharmaceutical storage complies with RDC 44/2010 (if dispensing medications)
- [ ] Annual ANVISA facility inspection documentation filed

## Staff & Safety Requirements

- [ ] All clinical staff licensed with their respective regulatory council (CFM, COREN, CRF, etc.)
- [ ] Staff immunization records current (influenza, hepatitis B — NR-32)
- [ ] PPE available and used per ANVISA and Ministry of Health protocols
- [ ] Incident reporting system active — adverse events registered in NOTIVISA
- [ ] Patient safety committee active (RDC 36/2013 for surgical/higher-risk clinics)

## Documentation & Audit Trail

- [ ] All compliance decisions documented with regulation reference and date
- [ ] Audit log of changes to patient records maintained
- [ ] Staff training records current for all compliance training
- [ ] Compliance review conducted quarterly and findings documented
- [ ] Annual self-assessment against ANS and CFM standards completed
